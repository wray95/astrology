# Planetary Exaltation & Debilitation (Vedic Astrology)

**Source:** Astrokarak — *"Exaltation & Debilitation of Planets in Vedic Astrology"*
(https://www.astrokarak.com/blog/exaltation-and-debilitation-of-planets-in-vedic-astrology).
Positions established in **Bṛhat Parāśara Horā Śāstra (BPHS)**, the foundational text of Parāśarī Jyotiṣa.

This is **foundational reference data** for accurate predictions: exaltation/debilitation is the
baseline "dignity" of every planet before house lordship, aspects, conjunctions and dasha are
even considered.

---

## Core Concepts

- **Exaltation (Uccha / Ucchabala):** the sign where a planet operates at *maximum potency*; results
  arrive at the upper end of its possible range.
- **Debilitation (Nīca / Nīcabala):** the sign in sharpest tension with the planet's nature; its
  expression is *constrained*, not destroyed. Results arrive at the lower end of what that planet's
  functional role allows.
- **Debilitation is a description of quality, NOT a verdict.** A debilitated planet "works harder"
  through resistant terrain — difficulty is real, but "working harder" ≠ "never arriving."
- **Symmetry:** each planet's debilitation sign is the sign **directly opposite** its exaltation
  sign, at the **same degree**. Max strength and min strength are polar expressions of the same nature.
- Neither exaltation nor debilitation **overrides the planet's fundamental role for a specific Lagna**
  (house lordship and placement still dominate outcome).
- **Rahu & Ketu** are *not* in this classical 7-planet table; their exaltation/debilitation vary by school.

- **Sun in Aries:** the Sun is singular authority, self-possessed, centrally visible. Aries is pure
  initiative, ruled by Mars (the Sun's friend) — "does not deliberate, it moves." No friction between
  the Sun's drive for singular expression and Aries's unimpeded forward action → maximum unobstructed
  potency at 10° Aries.
